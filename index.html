Public Speaking Tips (Andy Stanley)

ME – WE – GOD – YOU – WE Framework
  • ME: Open with your own story. Create tension the audience can feel.
  • WE: Widen the lens — this is everyone's story too.
  • GOD: Bring in Scripture. What does God say about this tension?
  • YOU: Give one specific, actionable next step.
  • WE: Close with collective vision — what changes if we all do this?
One Bottom Line
  • Every great sermon can be distilled into one memorable sentence.
  • Write your Bottom Line first — let it govern every other decision.
  • If you can't say it in a sentence, you don't know what you're preaching yet.
Create and Hold Tension
  • Introduce a compelling question or dilemma early.
  • Resist resolving the tension too quickly — let it breathe.
  • Resolution is the reward for patient listeners.
Engage Emotion + Intellect
  • Stories, humor, and vivid language reach the heart.
  • Scripture, logic, and evidence satisfy the mind.
  • The best sermons do both at once.
Practical Delivery Tips
  • Slow down when making your most important points.
  • Pause after the Bottom Line — let it land.
  • Make eye contact with individuals, not the crowd.
  • Move with purpose; stillness signals gravity.
  • End before they want you to.
